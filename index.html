My Skills

User Research

I determine meaningful insights from the research I conduct. This research includes surveys, qualitative interviews, and web traffic data.

Utilizing these insights allows me to set baseline metrics and define/refine features of websites or applications.

My research also includes analyzing website for design patterns using heuristic evaluations or evaluate their strengths and weaknesses using SWOT analyses.

Aside from my skills in the field, my degree also focused on research and analysis. This focus included statistical analysis and experimental design.

Sitemapping

By conducting audits on webpages and applications, I’m able to create a comprehensive content inventory. This inventory allows me to determine all of the linked items on a site and to where they all lead.

Using the information from my content inventories allows me to create cards based on that information to conduct a card sort. The card sort is one of the most important tools in my opinion for organizing information into a meaningful structure.

After completing the card sort, I am able to complete new sitemaps to better visualize the new organization of the information in it’s entirety.

User Flow

Through both non-UX and UX work, I have plenty of practice in designing both task and user flows to give a holistic representation of a user’s journey through the product.

Using these user flows not only gives a visual representation and organization to the process, but also allows myself and others to better empathisize with what a user will be doing as they navigate the product.

As I mentioned, I have experience outside of the UX field designing user flows as well. In several of my previous positions, I have designed training materials that cover flows that varied not only in scope, but also the field they impacted.

My Work

Designing a website for a startup

In a team of 5, I worked directly with stakeholders from a real startup to design a brand new website for them, from the ground up.

We conducted background research into their target market demographic in order to focus our designs on would be using the website.

Our end product was a functioning high-fidelity prototype for both a mobile and a desktop version of the website. This prototype aligned with our stakeholders’ busines needs, their branding requirements, while still being informative and fun.

This project was completed in a 2 week sprint using the agile method and task hand offs. During this course we were able to deliver a product that exceeded stakeholder expectations even with minimal guidance from the stakeholders.

Redesigning a government agency website

We picked out and redesigned the taskflow of a portion of the Department of Labor’s official website in a team of 5.

After determing our task flow, we conducted heuristic analyses on all of the pages in the workflow. We also conducted user tests to determine what users were struggling with in regards to navigation and copy legibility.

We reworked the navigation of the task flow and condensed unnecessary pages together to simplify the flow. Adjustments were then made to the copy of the pages to reduce the amount of jargon and to make it more readable.

A new UI style guide was also developed and implemented to add more visually compelling elements to the pages while making them more approachable to every day users.

Designing a travel application

As a solo project, I designed a travel iternerary app from scratch resulting in a working prototype.

I conducted user search via surveys and interviews to determine what would be useful features for the app. After the research, I analyzed the results and began ideating useful features.

Despite the limited scope for the first iteration of features, I mapped out the full userflow of the app in order to help with future iterations and expansions in features that would be displayed in the prototype.

A functionining low fidelity prototype was created and tested through a series of guerilla tests. The results were promising and the prototype received positive feedback.
